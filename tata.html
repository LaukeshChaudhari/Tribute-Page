Tribute to Sir Ratan Tata

Sir Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. He was born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai, India. Sir Ratan Tata is the son of Naval Tata, who was adopted into the family of Sir Ratanji Tata by his childless grandfather Sir Ratanji Tata.

Sir Ratan Tata graduated from Cornell University in 1962 with a degree in Architecture, and later completed the Advanced Management Program at Harvard Business School in 1975. He joined the Tata Group in 1962 and was appointed as the chairman of Tata Sons in 1991.

Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded globally, acquiring companies such as Jaguar Land Rover, Corus, and Tetley. Sir Ratan Tata is also known for his philanthropic work, particularly in the areas of education and healthcare. He has been honored with several awards and accolades, including the Padma Bhushan and the Padma Vibhushan, two of India's highest civilian awards.

Sir Ratan Tata retired as the chairman of Tata Sons in 2012, but continues to be involved in various Tata Group companies and serves on the board of several other organizations.
